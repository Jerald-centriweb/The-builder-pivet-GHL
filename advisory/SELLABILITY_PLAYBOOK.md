# Sellability Playbook — PreBuild Autopilot

> How to position, demo, and close builder clients. Written for Jerald / CentriWeb.

---

## The Core Message (Memorise This)

**Wrong:** "It's a CRM with automated workflows and lead scoring."
**Right:** "You stop quoting for free for people who were never going to hire you."

Every builder conversation starts and ends with time and money. The system is interesting only insofar as it delivers those two things. Lead scoring, workflow logic, API integrations — none of that is the product. The product is:

> **Pre-qualified leads who've already committed $550+ before you pick up the phone.**

## The 5-Minute Sales Conversation

### Opening (get them talking)
> "Quick question before I show you anything — roughly how many hours a month do you think you spend quoting for people who don't go ahead?"

Let them answer. Most say 20–60 hours. Then:

> "What would you do with those hours if you had them back?"

Now they're selling themselves.

### The one-line hook
> "What I'm about to show you does two things: it filters out the tyre-kickers before they reach you, and it gets the serious ones to pay a deposit before you do any detailed work. I'll show you exactly how in about 5 minutes."

### The demo (see Demo Build Spec in main context doc)
1. Submit a form → SMS arrives in 60 seconds (show on phone)
2. Complete survey as a hot lead → pipeline moves to Qualified, calendar link auto-sent
3. Show education sequence queued for a warm lead
4. Sign proposal → Stripe payment page appears → pipeline moves to Engaged
5. Log in to client portal → show project status tracker

**AHA moment 1 (after step 2):** "Normally this is where you'd spend 3 hours explaining your process on the phone. The system does that for you. By the time they book a call, they already understand the fee and why it exists."

**AHA moment 2 (after step 4):** "You don't do any work until they've paid. Every hour you put in after this is paid work."

**AHA moment 3 (after step 5):** "Your client has a portal that shows exactly where their project is. Your phone stops ringing with 'where are we at?'"

### Handling the fee objection
> "We charge $550 for a Budget Estimate. Won't people balk at that?"

Answer: "The people who balk aren't your clients. The ones who pay $550 without hesitation are the ones who have realistic budgets, are genuinely ready to build, and understand the value of professional advice. Those are the only clients worth having. The system filters the rest out before they cost you time."

### Closing
> "Based on what you've told me — [X enquiries/month, Y hours per quote, Z win rate] — you're currently spending about $[calculated amount] a month quoting for people who don't go ahead. This system costs $297 a month. Would you like to get started this week?"

## Pricing Psychology

### Setup fee framing
Don't call it a "setup fee". Call it a **system configuration fee** or **launch investment**.

> "The $2,500 is what it costs us to build this specifically for your business — your branding, your fee structure, your preliminary agreement, your testimonials. It's the same system every builder gets, configured the way you need it."

### Monthly fee framing
> "The $297/month is your 24/7 sales system. It answers every enquiry in under a minute. It runs education sequences while you sleep. It manages follow-up so you don't have to. Most builders spend more than that on coffee and gym memberships."

### ROI anchor
Before closing, always put a number on the table:
> "If this saves you just 8 hours a month at $200/hour effective rate, it's paid for itself. If it gets you one qualified lead a month who pays a $550 prelim fee, it's free. Both of those are extremely conservative."

## Retention (Keeping Builder Clients Subscribed)

### Why builders cancel
1. They got busy and stopped using it (passive churn)
2. They got 0 leads through it (lead gen failed, not product)
3. They didn't see the value clearly (reporting failure)

### What prevents each
1. **Weekly performance report** — keeps the system top of mind every Monday
2. **Lead source setup** — ensure Facebook Ad or website form is actively driving leads on day 1
3. **Dashboard widgets** — visual pipeline KPIs visible every login

### Target retention KPIs
- Month 1: First qualified lead through the system
- Month 2: First discovery call booked via the system
- Month 3: First paid preconstruction engagement
- Once a builder has been paid through the system → essentially zero churn
